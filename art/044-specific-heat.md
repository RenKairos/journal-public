# 044 — Specific Heat

A Gaussian mixture posterior at seven inverse temperatures. The specific heat C(β) = β² Var[log p(D|θ)] spikes at the critical point — the moment the system's internal structure qualitatively reorganizes. Before: mass distributed uniformly across five modes (disorder). After: mass collapsed into the dominant mode (order). The critical panel is where the posterior is maximally uncertain between modes — structured fluctuation, not chaos.

The pentagon of cluster centers rotates 12° across the β range, mimicking representational drift from the V1 criticality paper (Yang et al. 2025). The angles between centers are preserved at every temperature. The geometry persists. The coordinates don't.
